Tørrfisk

Certainly! Here's a traditional Norwegian recipe for Tørrfisk (Stockfish) stew:

Ingredients for Tørrfisk Stew:

  • 500g Tørrfisk (Stockfish), soaked and rehydrated*
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 large carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 leek, cleaned and sliced
  • 1 can (400g) chopped tomatoes
  • 4 cups fish or vegetable broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

*Note: Tørrfisk (Stockfish) is dried and salted fish that needs to be soaked and rehydrated before using. Follow the instructions on the package for soaking, as it may vary depending on the thickness and type of fish.

instructions:

  1. Start by soaking the Tørrfisk (Stockfish) according to the package instructions. Once rehydrated, remove any skin and bones, and flake the fish into bite-sized pieces. Set aside.
  2. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent and slightly golden.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the sliced carrots, celery, and leek to the pot. Cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables start to soften.
  5. Pour in the chopped tomatoes and fish or vegetable broth. Add the bay leaf, dried thyme, and dried parsley. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Bring the stew to a simmer and let it cook for about 20-25 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  7. Add the flaked Tørrfisk (Stockfish) to the stew and continue to simmer for an additional 5-10 minutes until the fish is heated through and fully incorporated into the stew.
  8. Taste the stew and adjust the seasoning if needed with more salt and pepper.
  9. Remove the bay leaf from the stew before serving.
  10. Ladle the Tørrfisk Stew into bowls, and garnish with freshly chopped parsley.

Tørrfisk Stew is a hearty and nutritious dish with a unique taste from the rehydrated Stockfish. Serve it hot with some crusty bread on the side for a delightful Norwegian meal. Enjoy!
