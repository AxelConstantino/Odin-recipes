Fårikål

Fårikål is a delicious traditional Norwegian dish that combines lamb and cabbage, cooked slowly to create a rich and comforting flavor. Next, I present the recipe to prepare Fårikål:

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg of lamb meat (preferably with bones) in chunks
  • 1 large cabbage (approximately 1 kg), cut into large pieces
  • 8 whole black peppercorns
  • 2 teaspoons of salt
  • 2 teaspoons of wheat flour
  • 1.5 liters of water
  • Optional: bay leaves and sprigs of thyme for additional flavor (according to preference)
  • Garnish: boiled potatoes to taste
Instructions:
  1. In a large, sturdy pot, place a layer of cabbage chunks at the bottom.
  2. Add a layer of lamb pieces on top of the cabbage and sprinkle some salt and black pepper over them.
  3. Repeat the process, alternating layers of cabbage and lamb until all the ingredients are in the pot.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix the wheat flour with a little water to make a paste. Add this paste to the pot and stir gently to distribute it.
  5. Pour the water over the mixture in the pot. If desired, you can add some bay leaves and a few sprigs of thyme to add more flavor to the dish.
  6. Bring the pot to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let it simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the lamb meat is tender and easily falls off the bone.
  7. During the cooking process, it's essential not to stir the mixture too often to prevent the cabbage pieces from breaking apart. Instead, gently shake the pot from time to time.
  8. When the Fårikål is ready, remove the bay leaves and thyme sprigs, if you used them.
  9. Serve the Fårikål hot on plates, accompanied by boiled potatoes.

Fårikål is a comforting and traditional meal that is especially enjoyed during the colder months in Norway. I hope you enjoy preparing and savoring this delicious dish!
